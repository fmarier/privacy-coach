Welcome to the Firefox Privacy Coach! This add-on will teach you about the privacy features that Firefox has to offer.

Privacy Preferences

Firefox allows you to customize preferences that affect your privacy. We've broken down this list of preferences to explain what they actually mean and help you figure out what setting is right for you.

  • Do Not Track

    DNT lets your browser tell websites that you do not want to be tracked.

    • Tell sites that I do not want to be tracked: Firefox will send an HTTP header that requests that sites disable tracking.
    • Tell sites that I want to be tracked: Firefox will tell sites that you are okay with being tracked.
    • Do not tell sites anything about my tracking preferences: Firefox won't tell sites anything about your tracking preferences.

  • Cookies

    Cookies let websites store small pieces of data in your browser to rememeber state when you navigate between webpages. However, this means they can also be used to track you.

    • Enabled: This means all websites can store cookies.
    • Enabled, excluding 3rd party: This means websites can store cookies, but their third-party content, such as embedded ads, won't be able to store cookies.
    • Disabled: Websites won't be able to store any cookies. Although this protects you from tracking, this will break a lot of websites.

  • Firefox Health Report

    FHR shares data with Mozilla about your browser health and helps you understand your browser performance.

    • Enabled: Firefox will send data to Mozilla's servers.
    • Disabled: Firefox won't send anything.

  • Telemetry

    Telemetry shares more detailed performance, usage, hardware and customization data about your browser with Mozilla to help us make the browser better.

    • Enabled: Firefox will send data to Mozilla's servers.
    • Disabled: Firefox won't send anything.

  • Crash Reporter

    The crash reporter lets you choose to submit crash reports to Mozilla to help us make the browser more stable and secure.

    • Enabled: Firefox will prompt you to send a report to Mozilla whenever your browser crashes. This can help the engineers at Mozilla make your browser, but it may include some personal data, such as URLs you've visited.
    • Disabled: Firefox won't ask you if you want to send a crash report.

  • MozStumbler

    The MozStumbler shares approximate Wi-Fi and cellular location with Mozilla to help improve our geolocation service.

    • Enabled: Firefox will send geolocation data to Mozilla's servers.
    • Disabled: Firefox won't send anything.

Other Privacy Features

In addition to cutomizable privacy preferences, Firefox also includes features that you can use to keep your data private.

  • Private Browsing

    You can create private tabs to browse the internet without saving any information about what pages you've visited. Learn more.

  • Guest Browsing

    A Guest Session lets someone else use Firefox on your Android device without giving them access to your personal info like passwords, bookmarks and history. Learn more.

  • Clear History and Other Data

    Firefox offers you control over your personal data, such as browsing history, passwords and more. You can conveniently save this data to your browser and delete it any time you want to. Learn more.

    In your privacy settings, you can also choose to clear private data every time you select "Quit" from the main menu.

HTTPS Search

To keep your searches private, you should make sure to use search engines that support HTTPS. We'll prompt you before you make a search that goes over HTTP, and we'll also warn you if you try to make an HTTP search engine your default.

You can change your default search engine at any time in the search settings page.
